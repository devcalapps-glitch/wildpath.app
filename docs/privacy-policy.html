Overview

WildPath is a camping trip planner that runs entirely on your phone. There are no WildPath servers, no user accounts, and no cloud sync. Everything you enter — your trips, gear lists, meal plans, emergency contacts — is saved locally on your device and stays there.

We built it this way on purpose. Your planning data is yours, and you shouldn't need to hand it to us to go camping.

📱

Information Stored on Your Device

The following data is saved in your phone's local app storage. It is encrypted using Android Keystore and is never transmitted to WildPath or any third party:

  • Your name and optional email address (entered during onboarding)
  • Trip details — destination, dates, GPS coordinates, and notes
  • Gear lists and meal plans
  • Budget entries
  • Emergency contact names and phone numbers
  • Camping style preferences
  • Pass and permit information, including permit photos taken with your camera
  • Notification preferences

If you uninstall WildPath, Android automatically removes all locally stored app data from your device. WildPath has no copy of it and cannot recover it.

🚫

What WildPath Does Not Do

To be direct about it:

WildPath does not:

  • Send your personal data to any WildPath server — we don't have one
  • Create or require a user account
  • Include analytics or crash-reporting SDKs
  • Show advertising of any kind
  • Sell, share, or license your data to third parties
  • Track you across apps or websites
  • Store location history or build a movement profile
🔑

Device Permissions

WildPath requests three permissions. Here is exactly what each one is used for:

📍
Location

Used only to center the map on your current position when you open a trip map view. Your device coordinates are not stored by WildPath and are not sent to any service unless you explicitly search for a campsite or load weather for a trip you have saved.

📷
Camera

Used to photograph passes and permits and save them to your trip. Photos are stored locally on your device only — they are never uploaded or transmitted.

🔔
Notifications

Used to send trip reminders and weather alerts to your device. All notification logic runs on-device. Your notification preferences are stored locally and can be changed or disabled at any time.

🌐

Third-Party Services

WildPath connects to four external services to power its search and weather features. These are the only external calls the app makes. Each service receives only the minimum information needed to do its job — no names, no account details, no emergency contacts.

Google Places API
Handles campsite and location search. When you type a destination, your search query and approximate coordinates are sent to Google to return matching results. Governed by the Google Privacy Policy.
Open-Meteo
Provides hourly and daily weather forecasts. When you view weather for a trip, the trip's stored coordinates are sent to Open-Meteo. Open-Meteo is a free, open-source weather API that does not require API keys or user tracking. See their terms.
National Weather Service (weather.gov)
Used to fetch active weather alerts for your trip area. Trip coordinates are submitted to the NWS public API. NWS is a U.S. government service and does not log or retain query data for commercial purposes. See the weather.gov disclaimer.
WeatherAPI
An optional additional source for weather alerts. If configured, trip coordinates are sent to WeatherAPI when fetching alert data. See the WeatherAPI Privacy Policy.

WildPath does not control how these providers handle request data on their end. Each provider's own privacy policy governs their data practices.

🔐

Data Security

Sensitive data stored in WildPath — including your profile information and emergency contacts — is encrypted using Android Keystore, the security system built into your device. This means even if someone accessed your phone's storage directly, they could not read the data without your device credentials.

Because WildPath stores everything locally and transmits nothing to our own servers, there is no WildPath database that could be breached. The security of your data is tied directly to the security of your device.

🗑

Data Retention and Deletion

Your data lives on your device for as long as the app is installed. You are in full control:

  • Delete individual trips, gear items, or expenses at any time from within the app
  • Clear all app data through your device's Settings → Apps → WildPath → Storage → Clear Data
  • Uninstall the app to remove all locally stored data automatically

WildPath holds no copy of your data. Once you delete it from your device, it is gone and cannot be recovered by us.

👶

Children's Privacy

WildPath is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has entered personal information into WildPath, clearing app data from the device settings will remove it entirely.
