Overview

WildPath is a camping trip planner that does not use user accounts or cloud sync. The app keeps your planning data on your device and does not maintain a developer-run trip database.

Some features do send limited data to third-party providers when you use them. For example, destination search may be relayed through a WildPath-managed serverless proxy to Google Places, weather screens send saved trip coordinates to weather providers, and opening directions hands coordinates to your chosen maps app.

🧭

Planning Responsibility

WildPath is a planning tool designed to help you organize information for your trip. It is not a substitute for your own judgment, local guidance, or emergency preparation.

Outdoor conditions, access rules, permits, trail status, weather, and rescue availability can change quickly. You are responsible for verifying your plans, checking official sources, and making sure you are adequately prepared before heading out.

📱

Information Stored on Your Device

The following data is saved on your device. WildPath does not store it on WildPath servers:

  • Your name and optional email address in encrypted secure storage
  • Trip details — destination, dates, saved GPS coordinates, and notes — in app preferences
  • Gear lists, meal plans, budget entries, emergency contacts, camping preferences, and notification settings in app preferences
  • Pass and permit information, including permit photos or picked files, in app-managed local files plus references stored by the app

If you use the in-app Get My Current Coordinates feature, WildPath reads your device's current GPS position in real time and shows it on screen for you. Those live coordinates are not automatically saved to WildPath storage unless you choose a feature that stores, copies, shares, or uses them for a trip.

If you uninstall WildPath, Android automatically removes all locally stored app data from your device. WildPath has no copy of it and cannot recover it.

🚫

What WildPath Does Not Do

To be direct about it:

WildPath does not:

  • Store your personal data in a WildPath cloud database or account system
  • Create or require a user account
  • Include analytics or crash-reporting SDKs
  • Show advertising of any kind
  • Sell your data, broker it, or share it with advertisers or analytics providers
  • Track you across apps or websites
  • Store location history or build a movement profile

WildPath does make limited requests to the feature providers listed below when you use search, weather, alerts, or maps functionality, including an optional WildPath-managed search proxy.

🔑

Device Permissions

WildPath requests three permissions. Here is exactly what each one is used for:

📍
Location

Used only when you choose location-based features, such as opening a trip map view or tapping Get My Current Coordinates on the Emergency screen. Your live device coordinates are not automatically stored unless you use a feature that saves them to a trip. Saved trip coordinates may later be sent to weather and alert providers when you load forecasts or alerts, or when background alert checks are enabled.

📷
Camera

Used to photograph passes and permits and save them to your trip. Photos are stored locally on your device only — they are never uploaded or transmitted.

🔔
Notifications

Used to send trip reminders and weather alerts to your device. All notification logic runs on-device. Your notification preferences are stored locally and can be changed or disabled at any time.

🌐

Third-Party Services

WildPath connects to external services to power its search and weather features. These are the only external calls the app makes. Each service receives only the minimum information needed to do its job — for example destination search text or trip coordinates, but not your name, email address, or emergency contacts.

WildPath Search Proxy
If enabled in your app build, destination search requests are sent first to a WildPath-managed serverless proxy hosted on Netlify, which forwards them to Google Places. The proxy is used to keep the Google web-service key off the device and is not intended to store your trip plans or build user profiles.
Google Places API
Handles campsite and location search. When you type a destination, your search query is sent to Google, either directly from the app or through the WildPath search proxy, to return matching results and place details. If you tap Open in Maps, coordinates are also handed off to Google Maps or your selected maps app. Governed by the Google Privacy Policy.
Open-Meteo
Provides hourly and daily weather forecasts. When you view weather for a trip, the trip's stored coordinates are sent to Open-Meteo. Open-Meteo is a free, open-source weather API that does not require API keys or user tracking. See their terms.
National Weather Service (weather.gov)
Used to fetch active weather alerts for your trip area. Trip coordinates are submitted to the NWS public API when you load alerts, and may also be checked periodically in the background if weather alerts are enabled. NWS is a U.S. government service. See the weather.gov disclaimer.
WeatherAPI
An optional additional source for weather alerts. If configured, trip coordinates are sent to WeatherAPI when fetching alert data. See the WeatherAPI Privacy Policy.

WildPath does not control how these providers handle request data on their end. Each provider's own privacy policy governs their data practices.

🔐

Data Security

Your name and email address are stored using encrypted secure storage on Android. Other planning data is kept in app-local preferences and files inside your device's app sandbox. Permit and pass attachments are stored as local app-managed files on the device.

WildPath does not maintain a cloud database containing your trips. If the optional search proxy is enabled, it forwards destination search requests but is not intended to store trip records. The security of your local data is tied primarily to the security of your device and the third-party feature providers you choose to use.

🗑

Data Retention and Deletion

Your data lives on your device for as long as the app is installed. You are in full control:

  • Delete individual trips, gear items, or expenses at any time from within the app
  • Delete permit or pass attachments from within the app where supported
  • Clear all app data through your device's Settings → Apps → WildPath → Storage → Clear Data
  • Uninstall the app to remove all locally stored data automatically

WildPath holds no copy of your data. Once you delete it from your device, it is gone and cannot be recovered by us.

Requests already sent to Google Places, Open-Meteo, weather.gov, WeatherAPI, or external map apps are governed by those providers' own retention and privacy practices.

👶

Children's Privacy

WildPath is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has entered personal information into WildPath, clearing app data from the device settings will remove it entirely.
